# Domain Profile — Electromovilidad y Política de Transporte

## Field

**Primary:** International Management / Business Economics — Sustainability & Mobility Transitions
**Adjacent subfields:** Energy Economics, Development Economics, Industrial Policy, Innovation Policy

---

## Paper Type

**Descriptive-Analytical Essay** (not causal inference)
- Goal: Diagnose barriers, compare policies, propose recommendations
- Method: Comparative case study + quantitative evidence from market data
- Standard: UAI CEMS Master thesis essay, APA citations, 8,000–9,000 words

## Common Analytical Strategies

| Strategy | Application | Key Requirement |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Comparative case study | Chile vs. Norway / China / Netherlands | Select comparable cases on key dimensions |
| Benchmarking | KPIs across countries (EV share, charger density) | Consistent definitions across sources |
| Policy mapping | Identify mechanisms → outcomes | Careful with attribution (correlation ≠ causation) |
| Scenario analysis | EV adoption trajectories for Chile | Transparent assumptions |

---

## Field Conventions

- Always cite IEA Global EV Outlook as primary data anchor for global EV statistics
- Distinguish between BEV (battery electric) and PHEV (plug-in hybrid) in all data
- Report EV market share as % of new vehicle registrations (not total fleet)
- Charging infrastructure: report both total units and density (per 100k population or per 100 EVs)
- Be explicit about data vintage (EV market moves fast — 2022 data is already stale)
- Norway comparisons: always note that Norway is an outlier (oil wealth, specific geography) — acknowledge limits of transferability
- China comparisons: acknowledge state-directed economy caveat

## Field-Specific Referee / Evaluator Concerns

- "¿Por qué Chile y no otro país de LatAm?" — must justify case selection
- "¿Son los casos de Noruega/China realmente comparables?" — must acknowledge differences and explain transferability logic
- "¿Los datos son actuales?" — EV market changes fast; use 2022–2024 data where possible
- "¿Hay postura propia o solo descripción?" — evaluators expect a clear argumentative stance
- "¿Las recomendaciones son factibles en el contexto institucional chileno?" — policy proposals must be grounded
